# Nutrition Plan (2 Weeks, Vegetarian)

## Daily Meal Template

### Breakfast (7:00-8:00 AM)
- **Protein Base**: Greek yogurt, eggs, protein smoothie, or tofu scramble
- **Complex Carbs**: Oats, whole grain toast, or quinoa
- **Healthy Fats**: Nuts, seeds, or avocado
- **Fruits/Vegetables**: Fresh or frozen fruits, spinach in smoothies

### Lunch (12:00-1:00 PM)
- **Protein**: Legumes, quinoa, eggs, or plant-based protein
- **Vegetables**: 2-3 servings of colorful vegetables
- **Complex Carbs**: Brown rice, whole grain pasta, or sweet potato
- **Healthy Fats**: Olive oil, nuts, or avocado

### Dinner (6:00-7:00 PM)
- **Protein**: Beans, lentils, tofu, tempeh, or eggs
- **Vegetables**: Large portion of mixed vegetables
- **Complex Carbs**: Quinoa, brown rice, or whole grain bread
- **Flavor Enhancers**: Herbs, spices, nutritional yeast

### Snacks (2-3 per day)
- **Morning**: Fruit with nuts or Greek yogurt
- **Afternoon**: Hummus with vegetables or whole grain crackers
- **Evening**: Herbal tea with a small portion of nuts or seeds

## Protein & Micronutrient Notes

### **Daily Protein Targets**
- **Women**: 46-75g daily
- **Men**: 56-91g daily
- **Active individuals**: 1.2-2.0g per kg body weight

### **Top Vegetarian Protein Sources**
1. **Legumes** (15-18g per cup): Lentils, chickpeas, black beans, kidney beans
2. **Tofu/Tempeh** (10-20g per serving): Versatile, absorbs flavors well
3. **Greek Yogurt** (15-20g per cup): High in protein, probiotics
4. **Eggs** (6g per egg): Complete protein, versatile
5. **Quinoa** (8g per cup cooked): Complete protein grain
6. **Nuts & Seeds** (4-8g per ounce): Almonds, hemp seeds, chia seeds
7. **Plant-based Protein Powder** (20-30g per scoop): Convenient for smoothies

### **Critical Nutrients for Vegetarians**
- **Vitamin B12**: Supplement recommended (2.4 mcg daily)
- **Iron**: Pair with vitamin C foods (citrus, tomatoes, bell peppers)
- **Omega-3s**: Flax seeds, chia seeds, walnuts, algae supplements
- **Zinc**: Pumpkin seeds, cashews, chickpeas
- **Calcium**: Leafy greens, fortified plant milks, tahini
- **Vitamin D**: Sun exposure, fortified foods, supplements if needed

### **Meal Prep Tips**
- **Batch cook grains**: Prepare quinoa, brown rice, and lentils in large batches
- **Pre-chop vegetables**: Store in glass containers for easy access
- **Make overnight oats**: Prepare 3-4 jars at once
- **Freeze smoothie packs**: Pre-portioned fruits and vegetables
- **Prepare snack portions**: Divide nuts, seeds, and dried fruits

## Recipe Details for Key Meals

### **Overnight Oats (Base Recipe)**
*Serves 1, prep time: 5 minutes*

**Ingredients:**
- 1/2 cup rolled oats
- 1/2 cup almond milk
- 1 tbsp chia seeds
- 1 tbsp almond butter
- 1 tsp honey or maple syrup
- 1/2 banana, sliced
- Pinch of cinnamon

**Instructions:**
1. Mix all ingredients except banana in a jar
2. Refrigerate overnight
3. Top with banana slices before serving
4. Can be made 3-4 days ahead

### **Three-Bean Chili (Batch Recipe)**
*Serves 6-8, prep time: 15 minutes, cook time: 30 minutes*

**Ingredients:**
- 1 can each: black beans, kidney beans, pinto beans (drained and rinsed)
- 1 large onion, diced
- 3 cloves garlic, minced
- 1 bell pepper, diced
- 1 can diced tomatoes (28 oz)
- 2 cups vegetable broth
- 2 tbsp olive oil
- 2 tsp cumin
- 2 tsp chili powder
- 1 tsp paprika
- Salt and pepper to taste

**Instructions:**
1. Heat oil in large pot, sauté onion and bell pepper (5 minutes)
2. Add garlic, cook 1 minute
3. Add spices, cook 30 seconds
4. Add tomatoes, beans, and broth
5. Simmer 20-25 minutes
6. Season with salt and pepper
7. Serve with cornbread or over rice

### **Quinoa Buddha Bowl**
*Serves 2, prep time: 15 minutes, cook time: 20 minutes*

**Ingredients:**
- 1 cup quinoa, rinsed
- 1 can chickpeas, drained and rinsed
- 2 cups mixed vegetables (broccoli, carrots, bell peppers)
- 2 cups spinach
- 1/4 cup tahini
- 2 tbsp lemon juice
- 1 clove garlic, minced
- 2 tbsp olive oil
- Salt and pepper to taste

**Instructions:**
1. Cook quinoa according to package directions
2. Toss chickpeas with 1 tbsp oil and seasonings, roast at 400°F for 15 minutes
3. Steam or roast vegetables until tender
4. Whisk tahini, lemon juice, garlic, and remaining oil for dressing
5. Assemble bowls with quinoa, vegetables, chickpeas, and spinach
6. Drizzle with tahini dressing

### **Simple Tofu Scramble**
*Serves 2, prep time: 5 minutes, cook time: 10 minutes*

**Ingredients:**
- 1 block firm tofu, drained and crumbled
- 2 cups fresh spinach
- 1/4 cup nutritional yeast
- 1 tsp turmeric
- 1 tsp garlic powder
- 2 tbsp olive oil
- Salt and pepper to taste

**Instructions:**
1. Heat oil in large pan
2. Add crumbled tofu, cook 3-4 minutes
3. Add spices and nutritional yeast, stir well
4. Add spinach, cook until wilted
5. Season with salt and pepper
6. Serve with toast or in a wrap

## Nutritional Guidance & Sustainable Habits

### **Building Sustainable Eating Habits**

#### **The 80/20 Rule**
- Aim to eat nutritiously 80% of the time
- Allow flexibility for social situations and cravings 20% of the time
- This prevents all-or-nothing thinking and promotes long-term success

#### **Mindful Eating Practices**
1. **Eat without distractions**: Put away phones and screens
2. **Chew slowly**: Aim for 20-30 chews per bite
3. **Check hunger cues**: Eat when moderately hungry, stop when satisfied
4. **Practice gratitude**: Appreciate the food and its journey to your plate

#### **Hydration Goals**
- **Daily target**: 8-10 glasses of water
- **Morning routine**: Start with 1-2 glasses upon waking
- **Pre-meal hydration**: Drink water 30 minutes before meals
- **Herbal teas**: Count toward fluid intake and provide antioxidants

### **Long-Term Success Strategies**

#### **Meal Planning Habits**
- **Sunday prep**: Dedicate 1-2 hours for weekly meal prep
- **Flexible planning**: Have backup options for busy days
- **Seasonal rotation**: Change up recipes based on seasonal produce
- **Social consideration**: Plan for dining out and social events

#### **Kitchen Organization**
- **Pantry staples**: Always keep basics stocked
- **Storage solutions**: Invest in glass containers for meal prep
- **Equipment essentials**: Good knife, cutting board, blender, and large pot
- **Spice collection**: Build gradually with versatile options

#### **Monitoring Progress**
- **Energy levels**: Notice how different foods affect your energy
- **Digestive health**: Pay attention to how your body responds to foods
- **Sleep quality**: Good nutrition supports better sleep
- **Mood stability**: Balanced nutrition helps regulate mood

### **Troubleshooting Common Challenges**

#### **"I Don't Have Time to Cook"**
- **Solution**: Batch cooking, slow cooker meals, and 15-minute options
- **Quick options**: Smoothies, overnight oats, pre-made salads
- **Time-saving tools**: Food processor, rice cooker, instant pot

#### **"I'm Always Hungry"**
- **Solution**: Increase protein and fiber at each meal
- **Timing**: Eat every 3-4 hours to maintain stable blood sugar
- **Hydration**: Sometimes thirst masquerades as hunger

#### **"It's Too Expensive"**
- **Solution**: Focus on whole foods over processed alternatives
- **Bulk buying**: Purchase staples in larger quantities
- **Seasonal eating**: Choose produce when it's most affordable

#### **"I Miss Meat"**
- **Solution**: Explore umami-rich foods (mushrooms, nutritional yeast, miso)
- **Texture variety**: Try different plant proteins and cooking methods
- **Gradual transition**: Allow time for taste preferences to adapt

- Include protein at every meal
